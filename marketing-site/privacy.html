Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-17

This privacy policy describes how Cabalmail (the "Service", operated by the entity identified in our Terms of Service) collects, uses, and protects personal information of users who create an account at this Cabalmail instance.

What we collect

Account information

  • Username chosen at signup.
  • Password, stored only as a salted hash by Amazon Cognito; the operator cannot recover or read it.
  • Mobile phone number, used solely to verify ownership of a phone you control and to enable account recovery. See "SMS messages" below.

Email content

Email messages received at addresses you create on the Service are stored on infrastructure under your operator's AWS account. The operator does not read your email. No third-party content scanning, advertising profiling, or machine-learning training occurs on message content.

Operational telemetry

Standard server logs (timestamps, IP addresses, HTTP request paths) are retained for security and abuse monitoring. Logs are not sold or shared with third parties.

SMS messages

By providing your mobile phone number at signup, you consent to receive transactional SMS messages from Cabalmail for the following purposes only:

  • Account verification at signup (a one-time numeric code).
  • Password reset when you request one (a one-time numeric code).
  • Sign-in verification when multi-factor authentication is enabled (a one-time numeric code).

We do not send promotional or marketing SMS. We do not share your phone number with third parties for marketing.

Message frequency: SMS is event-driven only. A typical user receives one SMS at signup and zero or more on subsequent password resets or sign-ins. There is no recurring or scheduled messaging.

Message and data rates may apply, depending on your wireless carrier's plan. Cabalmail does not charge for SMS; your carrier may.

To stop receiving SMS: reply STOP to any message from Cabalmail. You will receive a single confirmation message and no further SMS, with the exception that stopping SMS will disable SMS-based account recovery on your account.

For help: reply HELP to any message from Cabalmail, or email the operator contact address listed in the Terms of Service. You will receive a brief reply with support information.

The SMS originator number is operated by Amazon Web Services End User Messaging on the operator's AWS account. SMS delivery is performed by Amazon SNS; message content is generated by the Service and never includes account credentials, balances, or personally identifying detail beyond the one-time numeric code.

Phone number retention

Your phone number is retained for as long as your account exists. If you delete your account, your phone number is removed from the Cognito user pool within 30 days. We do not log SMS bodies; only delivery metadata (timestamp, originator, success/failure code) is retained, and that metadata is purged after 90 days.

Third parties

The Service runs on Amazon Web Services. AWS processes account data (Cognito), DNS (Route 53), email storage (EFS), and SMS delivery (End User Messaging / SNS) under its standard data processing terms. No other third-party services receive your account information.

Your choices

  • You may remove your phone number from the user pool at any time via the account settings UI (this disables SMS-based password reset).
  • You may delete your account at any time; deletion removes all email mailboxes and user pool records associated with your account.
  • You may exercise GDPR / CCPA rights (access, correction, deletion, portability) by contacting the operator. The operator address is in the Terms of Service.

Changes to this policy

Material changes to this policy will be announced via an in-app notice at next sign-in. Continued use of the Service after the announced effective date constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
